Clothing Swap

to Benefit Planned Parenthood & She Should Run

4834 7th Street NW, Washington DC
Saturday, May 30, 2020 at 1pm - 5:30pm

On the hunt for new clothes? Want to support Planned Parenthood and She Should Run? Looking for a way to do both fantastic things at once?!

You're in luck! Because the clothing swap to benefit charity is back for the fourth consecutive year!

It's $15 to sign up, then $1 per item of clothing you snag at the swap. Proceeds will be split between Planned Parenthood and She Should Run.

Planned Parenthood provides vital health services to women and families.

She Should Run is a nonpartisan nonprofit working to dramatically increase the number of women considering a run for public office. Which we need - now more than ever.

Sign-Up Deets

HOW TO SIGN UP:
  1. Please PayPal or Venmo $15 to Ceci De Robertis. Please include your name, email and the fact it is for the clothing swap in the message space. (Ex: “Ruth Bader Ginsburg for Clothing Swap, rbg@gmail.com”). This will count as your "sign-up".
  2. When you arrive, someone will be at the front door to check you in. When you leave, we will be accepting either cash, PayPal or Venmo for the number of items you are taking (remember, $1 per item, with the money split between Planned Parenthood and She Should Run).
More details:
  • You’re encouraged to come wearing leggings and a tank top for ease of changing in public. (we’ll have changing rooms, but only a few).
  • Please bring your own bags for the clothes you take with you. We will not be providing bags.
  • You’re also encouraged to bring wine or snacks to share! (we’ll also have a small amount on hand).
  • If you plan on paying in cash, please bring cash. We’ll have change, but small bills are always helpful.
  • We would love help transporting leftover clothes to a charity. If you have a car, please sign up on the sign up form all the way to the right. (We would love help even if you can’t make the swap).
FAQ -- PROVIDING ALL THE ANSWERS TO ALL THE QUESTIONS YOU MAY HAVE:

I didn’t sign up, but I still want to go. Is that OK?

If possible, we would like as many folks as possible to sign-up in advance. However, people coming day of are of course welcomed. The one catch is if there is no room inside, we may ask you to wait a little bit, but we’re hoping that it won’t get too overcrowded. The space is pretty big so it shouldn’t be a problem!!

What if I signed up but I can’t make it? Can I get my money back?

We’ll miss you for sure, however, we will sadly be unable to refund your money. However, know that your sign-up fee will go to a good cause. As a reminder, all money raised will be split between Planned Parenthood and She Should Run.

Will there be changing rooms?

There should be one or two changing areas. However, we strongly recommend that you come wearing a tank top and leggings in case you need to try things on in the open.

I like treats!

That’s not a question, but we’ll let it slide because we do as well! We will have some (a limited amount of) wine and snacks on hand and set up in the kitchen. Please feel free to bring some snacks or drinks to share with your fellow fashionistas and help create a fun and homey environment!

Do you need help setting up the swap?

Boy, do we! We’d love some in fact. If you’re able to help, please FB message JJ Jackson or email her at jenniferjackson33@gmail.com, and let her know.

Do you need help breaking down the swap?

BOY DO WE!! We especially need help transporting leftover clothes from the location of the swap to a local organization we are donating to. If you are able to help, please sign up on the sign up form or please FB message JJ Jackson or email her at jenniferjackson33@gmail.com, and let her know.

Is it too late to donate clothes?

NOPE! In fact, we’d love some more clothes. Donation details can be found below. Ideally, we’d love to get as many clothes in advance of the swap as possible so things are set up.

Can I donate mirrors, hangers or clothing racks?

Yes please! We could definitely use more mirrors and clothing racks. In addition, if you have easy-to-set-up bookshelves, that would also be helpful to fold clothes on.

Can I leave without paying? I am great with IOUs!

We’re sure you are, but we don’t want to track anyone down. Ergo, no one can leave without paying for their clothes. We will be accepting cash, PayPal and Venmo.

OK, this is all helpful. In summary, what should I bring?

We like things in lists, too, so here goes:

  • A bag to bring home your treasures in.
  • Small bills if you’re planning on paying in cash.
  • Your smartphone if you intend to pay via paypal or venmo. (preferred!)
  • A snack or wine to share.

And a reminder to wear leggings and a tank top for ease of trying on clothes.

As always, if you have any questions, please FB message JJ Jackson, post on the FB invite or email JJ at jenniferjackson33@gmail.com.

Clothing Drop Off Deets

Have clothing you want to get rid of?! GREAT! We would love to take them! Check out the FAQ below, as well as information on where and when to drop off clothes!

What are you looking for?

We are seeking women's clothing of any size. These clothes should be gently worn (no giant holes or stains, etc), and laundered.

We are also looking to collect accessories (purses, shoes, jewelry, belts, scarves, etc).

But I can't make the swap. Do you still want my clothes?

OF COURSE! We'd love to take your stuff off your hands!

What will you do with the clothing after the swap?

All clothes at the end of the swap will be donated to a local organization. If you would like to volunteer to take a load of clothing, please indicate this on the sign up sheet.

How do I donate?

Great! We'd thought you'd never ask. Please place all clothing (folded nicely) in bags separated into the following categories:

  • Skirts
  • Pants/shorts
  • Dresses
  • Tops
  • Sweaters
  • Jackets
  • Business suits
  • Accessories

Please label the outside of the bag with both the category and the size. So for example, one bag would say, "Skirts, sizes L to XL." Another bag would be "Sweaters, sizes XL to 2X." Please try to lump clothing in similarish sizes. (So put small tops in a different bag than XXL tops). This will help to expedite the day-of set up.

Please make sure shoes have the size on there somewhere. (You can always add a post-it note in the shoe if it's not readable on there).

I also have a ton of hangers and racks. Interested?

OH YES!!! We very much need hangers, clothing racks, mirrors, and shelves to display clothes! You can drop off all of these items at the below locations as well. NOTE: getting clothes and hangers BEFORE the event itself is a huge help.
